Complete Guide to Plate Heat Exchanger Selection & Maintenance

📅 May 18, 2026 ⏱ 14 min read

Pre-Selection Data Requirements

PHE selection is a systematic engineering process requiring accurate process parameters. The following information should be collected before sizing:

1. Fluid Parameters

Parameter Description
Hot side flow rate m³/h or kg/h
Cold side flow rate m³/h or kg/h
Hot side inlet temperature °C
Hot side outlet temperature °C
Cold side inlet temperature °C
Cold side outlet temperature °C
Media name Water, oil, steam, refrigerant, etc.
Media concentration Note if corrosive or particle-containing
Physical properties Density, specific heat, thermal conductivity, viscosity

2. Allowable Pressure Drop

  • Hot side allowable pressure drop (kPa or bar)
  • Cold side allowable pressure drop (kPa or bar)

3. Design Pressure and Temperature

  • Maximum working pressure (MPa)
  • Maximum working temperature (°C)
  • Whether special design conditions are required

4. Space and Connection Requirements

  • Installation space dimension constraints
  • Nozzle orientation requirements
  • Connection standards (flange standard, thread specification)

Heat Load Calculation

Basic Formula

Q = m · cp · ΔT

Where:

  • Q: Heat load, kW
  • m: Mass flow rate, kg/s
  • cp: Specific heat capacity, kJ/(kg·°C)
  • ΔT: Temperature difference, °C

Example

A cooling system:

  • Hot water flow rate: 50 m³/h
  • Hot water density: 1000 kg/m³ → mass flow rate = 50 × 1000 / 3600 = 13.89 kg/s
  • Hot water specific heat: 4.18 kJ/(kg·°C)
  • Hot water inlet: 80°C, outlet: 60°C

Heat load:

Q = 13.89 × 4.18 × (80 - 60) = 1161 kW

Log Mean Temperature Difference (LMTD)

For counter-current heat exchangers:

LMTD = (ΔT1 - ΔT2) / ln(ΔT1 / ΔT2)

Example:

  • Hot side: 80°C → 60°C
  • Cold side: 20°C → 40°C
  • Counter-current: ΔT1 = 80 - 40 = 40°C, ΔT2 = 60 - 20 = 40°C
  • LMTD = (40 - 40) / ln(40/40) → not directly calculable

When ΔT1 = ΔT2, LMTD = ΔT1 = ΔT2 = 40°C.

Temperature Approach

PHEs can typically achieve small approach temperatures (1–3°C), a key indicator of their high efficiency.

Core Selection Principles

1. Estimating Heat Transfer Area

A = Q / (K · LMTD · F)

Where:

  • A: Heat transfer area, m²
  • K: Heat transfer coefficient, W/(m²·K), PHEs typically 3,000–6,000
  • F: Correction factor, typically 0.8–0.95

2. Estimating Plate Count

N = A / A_single

Where A_single is the effective heat transfer area of a single plate.

3. Flow Configuration

PHE flow configuration directly affects heat transfer and pressure drop:

  • Single-pass/Single-pass: Both sides single pass, minimum pressure drop
  • Multi-pass/Multi-pass: Increasing passes raises velocity and heat transfer coefficient, but increases pressure drop
  • Single-pass/Multi-pass: One side single pass, one side multi-pass; for large flow differences

Daily Maintenance Essentials

Routine Inspection

  1. Check for external leaks or drips
  2. Monitor inlet/outlet pressures and temperatures
  3. Record pressure drop changes
  4. Check tightening bolts for looseness
  5. Observe for abnormal vibration in the base frame

Regular Cleaning

Cleaning frequency depends on media characteristics:

  • Clean water systems: 1–2 years
  • Cooling/river water: 3–6 months
  • Oil-containing or viscous media: 1–3 months
  • Food & beverage: after each production cycle

Cleaning methods:

  1. Backflushing: Reverse flush without disassembly
  2. Chemical cleaning: Circulate acid, alkali, or specialized cleaning agents
  3. Mechanical cleaning: Disassemble and use high-pressure water or brushes

Chemical Cleaning Precautions

  • Verify cleaning agent compatibility with plate and gasket materials
  • Control cleaning temperature and concentration
  • Thoroughly rinse with clean water after cleaning
  • Perform passivation treatment if necessary

Troubleshooting

Symptom Possible Cause Corrective Action
Reduced heat transfer efficiency Fouling, blockage, gasket aging/cross-flow Clean, replace gaskets
Increased pressure drop Fouling, blockage, plate deformation Clean, inspect plates
External leakage Gasket aging, loose bolts, plate cracks Tighten bolts, replace gaskets/plates
Internal leakage (cross-flow) Misaligned gaskets, perforation, cracks Reassemble, replace plates/gaskets
Abnormal vibration/noise Excessive flow, cavitation, inadequate support Adjust flow, vent air, reinforce support

Disassembly & Assembly Precautions

  1. Record compression dimension: Measure and record the A-dimension before disassembly
  2. Loosen bolts in diagonal sequence: Prevent plate pack deformation
  3. Mark plate sequence and orientation: Critical for correct reassembly
  4. Inspect plate and gasket condition: Replace if cracks or wear found
  5. Tighten bolts evenly in diagonal sequence: Gradually compress to specified dimension
  6. Perform pressure test: Check sealing after reassembly

Summary

PHE selection and maintenance directly affect heat transfer efficiency, service life, and operational safety. Selection must fully consider process parameters, media characteristics, space constraints, and economics. Operation requires diligent inspection, cleaning, and preventive maintenance. Through scientific management, PHEs can maintain high-efficiency, stable operation over the long term, delivering sustained value to the enterprise.
