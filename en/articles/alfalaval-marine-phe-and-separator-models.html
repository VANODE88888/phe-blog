Introduction

Alfa Laval (Alfa Laval) is the undisputed global leader in marine heat exchange and fluid separation equipment. On ocean-going vessels, virtually every ship is equipped with Alfa Laval plate heat exchangers or oil separators — from central cooling systems to fuel purification, from fresh water generators to lubricating oil treatment, Alfa Laval products cover nearly all core heat exchange and separation functions in the ship's engine room.

This article systematically introduces the common models of Alfa Laval marine plate heat exchangers and oil separators, helping shipowners, engineers, and equipment selection specialists quickly understand the technical parameters and application scenarios of each model.

2. Alfa Laval Marine Oil Separator — Common Models

2.1 What is a Marine Oil Separator?

A marine oil separator (purifier) uses centrifugal force to separate water, impurities, and solid particles from fuel oil and lubricating oil. On ships, purifiers are essential for maintaining fuel and lube oil quality:

  • Fuel oil purifier: Purifies heavy fuel oil (HFO) / marine diesel oil (MDO), removing water and ash to ensure proper engine combustion
  • Lubricating oil purifier: Purifies main/auxiliary engine circulating lube oil, removing wear metal particles and water

Alfa Laval dominates the global marine purifier market with a share exceeding 70%.

2.2 ALCAP Series — Flagship Fuel Oil Purifier

ALCAP (Alfa Laval Centrifugal Automatic Purifier) is Alfa Laval's latest generation fuel oil purifier, featuring the EPC 60 control unit for fully automatic operation and intelligent sludge discharge.

Common Models:

Model Capacity (HFO) Recommended Vessel Weight Control Unit

ALCAP F205X 2,400 L/h Medium cargo ships 380 kg EPC 60
ALCAP F305X 4,800 L/h Large bulk / tankers 520 kg EPC 60
ALCAP F505X 9,600 L/h VLCC / ultra-large 720 kg EPC 60

ALCAP Core Technology Features:

  1. Fully automatic sludge discharge: EPC 60 controller triggers discharge based on oil water content, no manual intervention required
  2. Adaptive separation: Can process both HFO and MDO simultaneously, switching without component changes
  3. Low sludge volume: Only a small amount of sludge is discharged, minimizing oil waste
  4. Remote monitoring: Supports Modbus protocol for integration with ship integrated monitoring systems

2.3 ALCAP L-Series — Lubricating Oil Purifier

The ALCAP L series is specifically designed for main and auxiliary engine lubricating oil purification. Processing capacity is smaller than fuel purifiers, but separation precision requirements are higher.

Common Models:

ALCAP L205X 1,500 L/h 5,000~15,000 kW 340 kg

Key Requirements for LO Purification:

  • Must remove wear metal particles above 5μm
  • Purified lube oil water content should be <0.1%
  • Must operate continuously, synchronized with main engine

Conclusion

Alfa Laval marine plate heat exchangers and oil separators are core equipment in modern ship engine rooms. Key selection considerations:

  1. Plate heat exchangers: Select M10/M15/M20/T20P based on heat transfer area requirements; titanium plates must be used for seawater applications; prioritize ClipGrip™ glue-free gasket systems
  2. Oil separators: Choose F-series fuel purifiers and L-series lube oil purifiers based on daily oil consumption; ALCAP automatic sludge discharge technology significantly reduces crew workload
  3. Fresh water generators: AQUA series plate-type FWGs are an ideal choice for small and medium vessel fresh water supply
  4. Maintenance: Regular plate and disc cleaning, periodic gasket and seal ring replacement ensures long-term stable operation

Alfa Laval maintains service centers at major global ports with timely spare parts availability — a brand that shipowners and engineers can trust.
