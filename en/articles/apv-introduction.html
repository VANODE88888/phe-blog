APV: A Century-Old Pioneer in Heat Exchange Technology

📅 Jun 15, 2026 ⏱ 8 min read

Brand History

APV was founded in 1910 in the United Kingdom, initially producing homogenizers before evolving into a world-renowned brand in food and heat exchange technology. In 1997, APV was acquired by the Danish SPX FLOW group, becoming a key heat exchanger brand within SPX FLOW.

APV has deep technical expertise in PHE, particularly excelling in food, dairy, and beverage hygienic applications.

Main Product Series

1. H Series Gasketed PHE

  • Models: H7, H10, H12, H17, H26, H32, H45, etc.
  • Features: Standard disassemblable design, flexible application
  • Applications: HVAC, general industrial cooling, district heating

2. Q Series

  • Models: Q030, Q055, Q080, Q110, etc.
  • Features: Next-generation high-efficiency plate design
  • Applications: HVAC, industrial process cooling and heating

3. SR Series Hygienic PHE

  • Models: SR1, SR2, SR3, SR6, SR9, SR14, etc.
  • Features: Compliant with 3A, EHEDG hygienic standards
  • Applications: Dairy, beverages, food, pharmaceutical

4. ParaFlow Series

  • Features: Semi-welded PHE
  • Advantages: Combines benefits of disassemblable and fully-welded designs
  • Applications: Refrigeration, ammonia systems, high-temperature service

5. R Series

  • Features: Large industrial PHE
  • Applications: Petrochemical, power, heavy industry

Technical Features

Safety Double-Wall Plate Design

For high-risk applications (such as food & beverage heating), APV offers double-wall plates. Even if one plate ruptures, media will not mix but leak visibly from the plate edge, allowing early detection.

Capitator Wide-Gap Plates

For media containing fibers or particles, APV provides wide-gap plate designs that reduce clogging risks and facilitate cleaning.

Multiple Seal Materials

APV gasket materials include:

  • Nitrile — oils and water
  • EPDM — water, steam
  • Viton — high temperature, chemical media
  • Neoprene — seawater, refrigerants

APV Applications by Industry

Dairy Industry

APV has deep involvement in dairy applications, including:

  • Milk cooling and pasteurization
  • Heating/cooling in yogurt and ice cream production
  • CIP cleaning systems
  • UHT ultra-high temperature sterilization systems

Food & Beverage

  • Heating/cooling of juices, beer, soft drinks
  • Heat exchange for viscous media such as syrups and sauces
  • Purified water systems

HVAC & Industrial

  • District heating substations
  • Chiller systems
  • Lubricating oil cooling
  • Process water cooling

Selection & Maintenance Advice

Identifying APV Equipment

APV equipment nameplates typically contain:

  • Model number
  • Serial number
  • Design pressure and temperature
  • Number of plates
  • Year of manufacture

Maintenance Key Points

  1. Regular cleaning: Based on media conditions, clean every 3–12 months
  2. Inspect gaskets: Check for hardening, cracking, deformation, or leakage
  3. Correct tightening: Reassemble to the compression dimension on the nameplate
  4. Spare parts selection: Use OEM or quality-compatible spare parts

Summary

APV is a veteran brand in the PHE industry with traditional strengths especially in hygienic applications. After the SPX FLOW acquisition, APV continues to innovate, with product lines covering gasketed, semi-welded, and hygienic types. For food, dairy, beverages, and general HVAC and industrial cooling applications, APV is a trustworthy choice.
