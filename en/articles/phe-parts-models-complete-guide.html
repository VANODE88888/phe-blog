Complete Plate Heat Exchanger Parts Model Guide: 14 Major Brands Quick Reference

📅 Jul 20, 2026 ⏱ 18 min read

Introduction

The core consumables of plate heat exchangers are plates and gaskets. During equipment maintenance and spare parts procurement, accurate identification of plate model is the first step — plates from different brands and series are not interchangeable, and gasket material must be selected based on media characteristics.

This article compiles common accessory models from 14 major global PHE brands: Alfa Laval, Tranter, APV, Sondex, GEA, Funke, Hisaka, API-Schmidt, Thermowave, Vicarb, LHE, Danfoss, CIPRIANI, and ACCESSEN. It serves as a practical quick-reference handbook for equipment selection, spare parts procurement, and maintenance replacement.

Plate and Gasket Selection Guide

1. Plate Material Selection

Material Suitable Media Characteristics
304 SS Fresh water, tap water Economical, widely used
316L SS Circulating water, mildly corrosive media Better corrosion resistance than 304
Titanium Seawater, high-chloride water Best corrosion resistance, higher cost
Hastelloy Strong acids and alkalis For special applications

2. Gasket Material Selection

Material Temperature Range Suitable Media Characteristics
NBR (Nitrile) -20°C to 110°C Water, hydraulic oil Good oil resistance, economical
EPDM -20°C to 150°C Steam, hot water Excellent aging and heat resistance
Viton (FKM) -20°C to 180°C Strong acids, oils Chemical corrosion resistant
HNBR -20°C to 150°C High-temperature oils Excellent high-temp oil resistance

3. Spare Parts Procurement Tips

  1. Confirm plate model: Each plate has the model stamped on it — check directly after disassembly
  2. Confirm plate count: Total plates = heat transfer area / single plate area; adding/removing plates requires recalculation
  3. Gasket matching: Gaskets from different models are not interchangeable, even within the same brand's different series
  4. Frame dimension: After replacing plates, verify the tightening dimension (A-value) matches the nameplate
  5. Batch consistency: Replace all gaskets in the same batch to prevent uneven aging and leakage
